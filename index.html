The Firefighters Charitable Foundation

A Responsive Redesign of a Non-Profit Website

I helped a nonprofit bring awareness to their mission through the introduction of an updated navigation sytem and reorganization of their website.

ROLE

Project Manager, UX Researcher, and UI Designer within a team of three

TIMELINE

Three Weeks

TOOLS

Miro, Figma, Whimsical, Google Suite, and Zoom

The Problem

The Firefighters Charitable Foundation was intended to provide financial support to people impacted by fire and disaster, prevention programs, and volunteer fire departments. We have observed our website does not provide a local connection for people to get involved, causing users to go to other local organizations. How might we help people connect to their local chapters so they can tangibly see the value of their contributions to others and themselves?

Our Solution

We believe providing local options and highlighting the positive impacts of donations to people who are interested in getting involved will achieve an increase of contributions to the foundation and thus help more people in need.

Diving In

User Interviews & Surveys

Starting off, I asked myself a few initial questions. Who is the primary user? What kind of goals do they have? How would someone find this organization? After interviewing seven participants and receiving 25 responses from our Google Survey via LinkedIn later on in the process, it became evident that my initial questions were just the beginning. Our research participants identified two main themes: a connection to local organizations and a simple, tangible way to see the impact of their involvement.

It surprised us that 77% of those surveyed wanted to support their local organization rather than a national organization. This presented a unique challenge since we chose a national organization!

Meet our User, Sherrie

We developed Sherrie Carson, a local teacher, to guide us through our redesign. Through our interviews, we iterated Sherrie Carson to better reflect those most impacted by fire disaster.

Sherrie's focus on her local community shifted how we approached our redesign for the Firefighters Charitable Foundation. We needed to create ties to local fire departments while staying true to our nationally based organization.

I Like, I Wish, What If

We used affinity mapping to identify not only the general scope of the Firefighters Charitable Foundation, but to decide which direction we wanted to take. This was a form of brainstorming during our research period that proved to be very helpful in setting up the foundation for the rest of our process.

As we paired down our affinity diagram, the central themes that users kept bringing up were connecting with local organizations and a simple, tangible way to see the impact of their involvement.

Development

Heuristic Evaluation

The Firefighters Charitable Foundation needed some modernization, a refresher on hierarchy, and revamping how users saw the impact of their donations. Based on our research, we also needed to implement a way for users to connect to their local community. As we went through a Heuristics evaluation, our key insights were the following:

  • Modernize the aesthetic of the website
  • Alphabetize and simplify navigation categories
  • Create a way for users to get involved on a local level through a national organization
  • Show the impact of donations

Information Architecturue

User Flow

To better understand how we would construct the core experience for the Firefighter Charitable Foundation, I designed a user flow. The user flow helped my team focus more on the experience and needs of the user and less so on the details that we would solidify later on. It also allowed us to communicate the entries and exits more clearly so we would have a better understanding moving forward. For example, we identified that the only way to donate was to go through AmazonSmile, which took users away from the site.

Card Sorting to Site Map

Unpopular opinion, I love to card sort. In card sorting, we found that the only way to digitally donate to the Firefighters Charitable Foundation was by leaving the website and going to AmazonSmile. In our iterated site map, we priortized keeping users on the wesbite and creating two ways to donate within the site itself.

Design

Style Guide

Heading into our style guide, we wanted to evoke feelings of dependability. We opted for a classic firefighter red and a charcoal color for our color palette to stick true to our firefighter roots.

After some research, we decided to do a mix of illustrations and pictures to convey our story better and help the user engage with the material in a more constructive way.

Wireframes to Mid-Fidelity

One of our biggest challenges while wireframing and prototyping was taking a national organization giving users the opportunity to connect locally. As we moved through low and mid-fidelity, we continually asked ourselves, does this reflect the data we've collected? The two biggest things we learned from prototyping were keeping it simple and consistent.

User Testing

When we conducted our user tests, we received constructive feedback about a lack of consistency with button size and drop shadow as well as comments on our color palette. We also received positive feedback on our Thank You page and the simplicity of the user flow.

We tested often, failed often, and kept going.

Our key insights from our low and mid-fidelity prototypes were that users wanted a simple and easy way to “Get Involved” with their community, especially as fire-disaster becomes a more prevalent issue.

Takeaways and Next Steps

We went from user research to defining and ideating, from wireframes to mid-fi to a heavily iterated high-fi within our three-week time frame.

For most of us, this was our first time working with each other, and we needed to learn each other's communication, working, and design styles quickly to find a clear path forward. This project was a lesson in collaboration and really highlighted that everyone has something to bring to the table. It was good practice for the real world!

For our next steps, I would like to build out mobile to ensure a consistent experience from desktop to mobile.
